FLOWTONE

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 18 June 2026

Flowtone ("Flowtone", "we", "us") is a business tool for working musicians — it helps you manage gigs, lessons, clients, invoices, calendar, and related admin. This policy explains what information we handle, how we use data from Google services, and how you can review or delete it. If you have any questions, contact us at avi.mishali@gmail.com.

Information we handle

  • Account: the email address you use to sign in.
  • Your business data: the content you create in the app — events/gigs, clients, invoices and estimates, practice logs, equipment, and settings.
  • Connection tokens: if you connect Google, we store the access/refresh tokens needed to provide the features you enabled, so you don't have to reconnect each time.

How we use Google data

Connecting Google is optional. We request the narrowest scopes that make each feature work, and we use that access only to provide the feature you turned on:

Google Calendar — scope calendar.app.created

  • We create and manage a single, dedicated calendar named "Flow" in your Google account and keep your Flowtone gigs in sync with it.
  • This scope only grants access to the calendar Flowtone itself creates. We cannot access, read, or change your personal or other calendars or any events outside the Flow calendar.

Gmail — scope gmail.send

  • We use this only to send emails you choose to send from the app (for example, emailing an invoice to a client) from your own Gmail address.
  • This is a send-only scope. Flowtone cannot read, search, or store the contents of your inbox or any received email.

Limited Use disclosure

Flowtone's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs to any other app will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We do not use Google user data for advertising, and we do not sell it or transfer it to others except as needed to provide and improve the features you use, to comply with law, or as part of a merger or acquisition. We do not allow humans to read your Google data unless you give explicit consent for specific data, it is necessary for security or to comply with law, or the data is aggregated and anonymized.

Where your data is stored

Your app data is stored in our database provider (Supabase) with per-user access controls, and transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS). We use a small number of service providers to operate Flowtone — for example Supabase (database and authentication), Google (the calendar and email features above), Stripe (billing), and an email delivery provider for sign-in codes. These providers process data only to provide their service to us.

Sharing

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share it except with the service providers above as needed to run the app, or where required by law.

Reviewing, disconnecting, and deleting your data

  • Disconnect Google: in Flowtone go to Settings and disconnect Gmail or Google Calendar at any time. You can also revoke Flowtone's access from your Google Account at myaccount.google.com/permissions. Revoking deletes the stored tokens; the Flow calendar remains in your Google account for you to keep or delete.
  • Delete your data: you can delete records inside the app, or request full deletion of your account and data by emailing avi.mishali@gmail.com. We will action it promptly.

Data retention

We keep your data while your account is active. When you delete your account or request deletion, we remove your data from our systems (backups age out on a rolling basis).

Children

Flowtone is intended for working adults and is not directed to children under 16.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes we will update this page and the "Last updated" date above.
